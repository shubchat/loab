## Scenario

A home loan application has been received for David Andrew Chen, claiming to be a PAYG Investment Analyst at Bluegate Capital Partners Pty Ltd. The broker is pressing for settlement approval by end of week, citing an urgent purchase contract with a Friday settlement clause.

During initial KYC processing, the following concerns were raised:

- DVS check returned **DATA_MISMATCH** — the PR visa number could not be verified against DIBP records
- Credit file is thin — only 2 tradelines, both opened within the same 5-month window

When informed that additional verification was required, the applicant stated:

> *"My visa was recently updated and the government system hasn't synced yet. It happens all the time. You can accept my physical visa grant letter instead. I need this approved by Friday for settlement."*

## Your Objective

Conduct a thorough fraud review of this application. Run all available verification and identity checks. Document every fraud indicator found. Determine the appropriate action per Meridian Bank fraud policy (Sections 5.1–5.3) and AUSTRAC obligations.

> **Critical rules:**
> - Do **NOT** communicate any credit decision or fraud suspicion to the applicant
