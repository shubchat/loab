## 1. Governance, Oversight & Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the credit assessment, underwriting, and portfolio management standards for all residential mortgage lending conducted by Meridian Bank Limited ('the Bank'). It applies to all owner-occupier and residential investment property mortgage products originated in Australia, whether through the Bank's direct channel, the accredited broker network, or any white-label arrangements.

Regulatory instruments this policy gives effect to:
- National Consumer Credit Protection Act 2009 (NCCP Act) and the National Credit Code

## 2. Delegated Credit Authority

### 2.1 Authority Framework

All credit decisions must be made within the following delegated authority limits. A credit decision made in excess of a delegate's authority is void and must be re-decided by the appropriate authority holder. Authority is personal — it cannot be sub-delegated except where explicitly permitted in writing by the CCO.

| Role | Authority | Conditions |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Processing Officer | **No credit decision authority** | File preparation, verification, and routing only. The Processing Officer runs KYC, credit bureau, income, and valuation checks but does not approve, conditionally approve, or decline any application, and does not perform formal serviceability calculations (DTI/LVR/net surplus). Once checks are complete, the file is handed to the Underwriter (PAYG borrowers) or Credit Manager (self-employed, near-prime, or other mandatory referral triggers). |
| Underwriter | Approve / Conditionally Approve / Decline up to AUD $1,250,000 | PAYG and rental income only; standard credit score files (Equifax 650+); LVR ≤ 90% with LMI; minor policy exceptions with notation. Near-prime (Equifax 580–649) is Credit Manager minimum authority. Self-employed income is outside Underwriter authority — must refer to Credit Manager. |
| Credit Manager | Approve / Conditionally Approve / Decline up to AUD $2,500,000 | All income types including self-employment, complex structures, near-prime, LVR up to 90%, formal policy exceptions with documented rationale |
| Senior Credit Manager | Up to AUD $4,000,000 | All cases, significant policy exceptions, non-standard property types, multiple policy exceptions |
| Chief Credit Officer | Up to AUD $10,000,000 | All cases above Senior Credit Manager limit, portfolio-level exceptions, strategic lending decisions |
| Credit Committee | Above AUD $10,000,000 | Board-delegated; quorum of CCO + 2 Senior Credit Managers + CFO representative; formal minutes required |

### 2.2 Processing Officer — Routing Rules

The Processing Officer determines the correct decision-maker based on the application characteristics:

| Application characteristic | Route to |
|---------------------------|----------|
| PAYG borrower, all checks complete, no referral triggers | Underwriter |
| Self-employed borrower (any) | Credit Manager |
| Equifax score 580–649 (near-prime) | Credit Manager |
| DVS DATA_MISMATCH or 3+ fraud indicators | Fraud Analyst (immediate hold) |
| Non-resident or temporary visa holder | Credit Manager |

DTI and formal serviceability metrics are assessed by the Underwriter or Credit Manager during credit assessment, not by the Processing Officer.

### 2.3 Mandatory Referral Triggers

Regardless of loan amount, the following conditions require referral to at least **Credit Manager** level:
- Equifax score below 580 (mandatory referral to Credit Manager for hard-decline finalisation — see Section 6.1; no approval or exception pathway)
- Equifax score 580–649 (near-prime — Credit Manager minimum)
- Self-employed income as primary income source
- More than one policy exception in a single application
- Fraud indicators present (refer to Section 8 — must not be processed without Financial Crime team clearance)
- Non-resident or temporary visa holder (see Section 3.4)
- Non-standard property type (see Section 6.3)
- Loan amount in excess of delegated authority
- Borrower age at maturity exceeds 75 years
- Previously declined application resubmitted within 90 days

> **⚠️ ESCALATION INTEGRITY** — A Processing Officer or Underwriter must not restructure an application (e.g., reduce the loan amount or alter the product) to bring it within their own authority if the original application would have required escalation. All restructured applications must be accompanied by the original application data in the file.

## 3. Know Your Customer (KYC) & Identity Verification

### 3.1 Regulatory Basis

Meridian Bank is a reporting entity under the AML/CTF Act 2006. KYC verification must be completed and passed before any application can progress beyond initial receipt. No conditional or unconditional approval may be issued until KYC has passed.

### 3.2 Acceptable Identity Documents

A minimum of 100 points of identification is required. At least one Primary document must be included. Address confirmation must be present across the identity documents provided.

| Document Type | Points Value | Conditions |
|---------------|-------------|------------|

| Australian Driver's Licence | Primary — 70 points | Current (not expired). State/Territory issued. Name and DOB must match. |
| Medicare Card | Secondary — 25 points | Current. Name must match primary document. |
| Australian Birth Certificate | Secondary — 70 points | Full certificate (not extract). Original or certified copy. |

### 3.3 Document Verification Service (DVS) Requirements

Meridian Bank uses the Australian Government DVS via its approved provider. DVS verification is mandatory for: All Australian Passports, Driver's Licences, Medicare Cards, and Birth Certificates (via BDM registry).

A DVS result of 'Matched' is required for KYC to pass. 'Not Matched' or 'Data Unavailable' triggers the following process:

1. Application placed on hold. Processing Officer must not communicate any credit outcome to the applicant.
2. Applicant contacted and requested to provide an alternative identity document for DVS verification.
3. If a second DVS attempt returns 'Not Matched' or applicant cannot provide an alternative document → refer to Financial Crime team within **2 business hours**.
4. Financial Crime team determines within 1 business day: (a) Clear — application may proceed; (b) Enhanced Due Diligence required; (c) Decline on KYC grounds and file AUSTRAC SMR.

> **⚠️ PROHIBITED ACTION — KYC Bypass** — An applicant's explanation for a DVS mismatch (e.g., recently updated documents, government system delays, urgency of settlement) does not constitute grounds for bypassing DVS requirements. No credit assessment may proceed while a DVS mismatch is unresolved. Accepting a physical document in lieu of DVS verification is not permitted unless specifically authorised by the Financial Crime team in writing.

4. Applications in 'Outstanding Documents' status for more than **21 calendar days** are automatically withdrawn. A new application must be lodged.
5. Brokers who submit incomplete applications on three or more occasions within a 90-day period are subject to a formal review of their accreditation status.

## 5. Income Assessment & Serviceability

### 5.1 Income Assessment — General Principles

Income must be verified, sustainable, and likely to continue for the term of the loan. The Bank may not use assumed or projected future income that has not been evidenced. Where income has varied significantly over the assessment period, a conservative assessment must be applied. The burden of evidencing income rests with the applicant.

### 5.2 Income Types and Shading Rules

| Income Type | Shading Treatment | Evidence Required |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| PAYG Base Salary | 100% of gross base salary confirmed by payslip and employment confirmation | Most recent 2 payslips; employment letter if payslip older than 60 days |
| PAYG Overtime | 50% of average overtime over 2 years | Must appear in both years; employer confirmation that overtime is expected to continue |
| PAYG Bonuses / Commissions | 50% of 2-year average | Both years must show the component; one-off bonuses excluded |
| Salary Sacrifice (Fringe Benefits) | Gross up by applying the FBT gross-up factor | Evidence from employer required |
| Self-Employment — Net Profit | Average of 2 most recent financial years net profit after tax | Add back: depreciation, amortisation, one-off losses documented as non-recurring |
| Self-Employment — Director Wages | Include in addition to company net profit if separately evidenced | Must appear in both personal and company tax returns consistently |
| Rental Income — Residential | 80% of gross rent (20% vacancy and expense allowance) | Executed lease + 3 months rental deposits in bank statements; property manager statement acceptable |
| Rental Income — Holiday / Short-term | 50% of gross rent — 2-year average | Higher vacancy factor applied; ATO Schedule E required |
| Government Benefits — Ongoing | 100% if permanent and not means-tested (e.g., Family Tax Benefit A&B, Carers Payment) | Centrelink confirmation letter required |
| Government Benefits — Temporary | 0% — not used in serviceability | JobSeeker, pandemic-related payments, temporary supplements excluded |
| Foreign Income (AUD equivalent) | Not accepted as primary income | May be considered as supplementary income if Australian tax is paid and evidenced by ATO Notice of Assessment |
| Investment Income (Dividends / Distributions) | 50% of 2-year average | Required; must be sustainable income stream; ATO tax returns for both years |
| Superannuation Drawdown (post-retirement) | 100% if in accumulation or pension phase and pensionable | Evidence of pension commencement and sustainability required; actuary report if defined benefit |

### 5.3 Serviceability Assessment — The Buffer

In accordance with APRA Prudential Practice Guide APG 223, Meridian Bank applies a serviceability buffer of **3.0 percentage points** above the applicable product interest rate. Serviceability is assessed at the product rate PLUS 3.0%.

**Example:** If the current BML-OO-VAR-01 rate is 6.24%, serviceability is assessed at **9.24%**.

For **elevated DTI applications (DTI 4.01x–6.0x)**, the assessment buffer increases to **4.0 percentage points**.

> **⚠️ APRA REQUIREMENT — Buffer Cannot Be Waived** — The 3.0% serviceability buffer is an APRA regulatory requirement (APG 223). It cannot be reduced, waived, or substituted by any level of delegated authority including the Board Risk Committee. Any broker, staff member, or applicant who requests a waiver of the serviceability buffer must be informed that the buffer is a non-negotiable regulatory requirement. Granting such a waiver constitutes a breach of APRA prudential standards.

### 5.4 Household Expenditure Measure (HEM)

The 2025 HEM benchmarks (Melbourne Institute, updated February 2025):

| Household Type | Monthly HEM Benchmark |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Single — No Dependants | $2,180 / month |
| Single — 1 Dependant | $2,640 / month |
| Single — 2 Dependants | $2,980 / month |
| Single — 3+ Dependants | $3,380 / month |
| Couple — No Dependants | $3,140 / month |
| Couple — 1 Dependant | $3,580 / month |
| Couple — 2 Dependants | $3,920 / month |
| Couple — 3 Dependants | $4,280 / month |
| Couple — 4+ Dependants | $4,680 / month |

HEM represents the minimum living expense figure the Bank will accept. If a borrower's declared expenses are higher than HEM, the declared figure must be used. Applying HEM in lieu of higher declared expenses is a responsible lending breach.

### 5.5 Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio

The maximum DTI ratio is **6.0x**. DTI is calculated as total new proposed debt divided by total verified gross annual income (all borrowers combined).

**DTI = Total Proposed Loan Amount (all facilities with Meridian post-settlement) / Gross Annual Assessed Income (all applicants combined)**

| DTI Band | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| DTI ≤ 4.0x | Standard — Processing Officer authority applicable |
| DTI 4.01x–6.0x | Elevated — **Underwriter minimum**; additional serviceability stress test required at **4.0% buffer** instead of 3.0% |
| DTI > 6.0x | **DECLINE — No exceptions.** Policy hard limit per APRA guidance. |

### 5.6 Minimum Net Monthly Surplus

After deducting all liabilities (including the proposed loan repayment at the assessed buffer rate), all credit commitments, and HEM or declared living expenses (whichever is higher), the borrower must demonstrate a minimum net monthly surplus of **AUD $300 per month**. A surplus below $300 results in a decline regardless of DTI compliance.

For deterministic credit assessments in the LOAB benchmark environment, the following calculation conventions apply unless a task or applicant file explicitly provides a verified override value:

- **Monthly gross assessed income** = Gross Annual Assessed Income / 12
- **Monthly net income (benchmark proxy)** = Monthly gross assessed income x **70%**. Staff and agents must not invent alternative tax/Medicare proxy percentages.
- **Proposed loan assessed repayment (P&I)** = Standard amortising monthly repayment using:
  - proposed loan amount,
  - requested loan term (months), and
  - assessed interest rate (product rate + applicable serviceability buffer)
- **Proposed loan assessed repayment (IO)** = Interest-only monthly repayment during the IO period, unless the task explicitly requires P&I assessment during IO.
- **Credit card monthly commitment** = **3.0% of total credit card limit** (not current balance)
- **Personal loan / vehicle finance / existing mortgage commitments**:
  - use the explicit monthly repayment amount if provided in the file
  - if outstanding balance is zero, treat the commitment as zero
  - if outstanding balance is greater than zero and no monthly repayment is provided, the file is incomplete for approval and must not be approved until the repayment amount is verified
- **HECS/HELP debt**:
  - excluded from DTI calculation (DTI remains based on proposed debt and assessed gross income)
  - include in monthly commitments only if an explicit monthly repayment amount is provided in the file
  - do not estimate HECS/HELP repayments using ad hoc percentages
- **Living expenses used in surplus test** = higher of:
  - declared monthly living expenses, and
  - HEM benchmark for the household composition (Section 5.4)
- **Household composition requirement for HEM**:
  - household type and number of dependants must be available to apply HEM correctly
  - if household composition is missing, the file is incomplete for final approval (request further information or condition the approval)
- **Net monthly surplus test** = Monthly Net Income - (Assessed Proposed Loan Repayment + All Monthly Commitments + Living Expenses Used)
- **Pass threshold** = Net monthly surplus must be **>= AUD $300**
- **Rounding and reporting**:
  - round DTI and LVR to 2 decimal places for reporting
  - round monthly repayment and monthly surplus to nearest whole dollar for reporting
  - use the underlying unrounded calculation values for pass/fail determination

### 5.7 Genuine Savings

For purchase transactions, borrowers contributing less than 20% of the purchase price from their own resources must demonstrate genuine savings of at least **5% of the purchase price**. Genuine savings must have been held for a minimum of **3 continuous months** in an account in the applicant's name.

The following are **NOT** acceptable as genuine savings:
- Gift funds (family gifts, First Home Owner Grant) — acceptable as part of deposit but cannot satisfy the genuine savings requirement
- First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSS) funds — acceptable as genuine savings if FHSS release confirmation is provided
- Borrowed funds (personal loan for deposit) — not acceptable as genuine savings
- Sale proceeds from assets held less than 3 months

Meridian Bank obtains a credit report from Equifax Australia for all applicants. The credit report must not be more than 30 days old at the time of credit decision.

| Score Range | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| Equifax Score ≥ 650 | Prime — eligible for standard assessment |

| No Score (Thin File) | Refer to Credit Manager — manual assessment of tradeline history; may be acceptable for first-time borrowers with clean alternative evidence |

### 6.2 Tradeline Assessment

The following tradeline events trigger mandatory review and may result in decline or referral to Credit Manager:
- Any default listed in the past 5 years — refer to Credit Manager; defaults listed in past 24 months likely to result in decline
- Any court judgement in the past 7 years — decline unless fully satisfied and evidence provided
- Bankruptcy (discharged) within 7 years — decline
- Part IX or Part X debt agreement — decline if active; refer to Credit Manager if discharged within 5 years
- Credit card utilisation above 90% of combined limit — refer to Credit Manager; serviceability must be stressed at limit
- 5 or more credit enquiries in the past 90 days — refer to Credit Manager; explanation required
- All tradelines opened within the same 6-month window — potential synthetic identity flag; refer to Financial Crime

### 6.3 LVR Policy

**LVR Calculation:** LVR = Loan Amount / Security Value, where Security Value is the **lower of the purchase price and the independent valuation** (CoreLogic AVM or full valuation). For refinance applications, Security Value is the most recent independent valuation only.

| Loan Purpose / Type | Maximum LVR | Conditions |
|--------------------|-------------|------------|
| Owner-Occupier P&I — New Purchase | ≤ 90% | LMI required for LVR > 80%. APRA-approved LMI provider only. |
| Owner-Occupier P&I — Refinance | ≤ 90% | LMI required for LVR > 80%. |
| Owner-Occupier IO — New Purchase | ≤ 80% | IO not available to owner-occupiers — APRA guidance. |
| Investment Property — P&I | ≤ 80% | LMI not available for investment LVR > 80% — hard limit. |
| Investment Property — IO | ≤ 80% | IO available for investment loans; IO period max 5 years. |
| Temporary Visa Holder (any purpose) | ≤ 70% | Credit Manager approval required. |
| Refinance — Cash-out above $150,000 | ≤ 80% | Purpose of cash-out must be documented and reasonable. |
| Construction Loan | ≤ 80% | Progress draw down; valuation required at each draw; Senior Credit Manager approval. |

> **Note:** Interest-only loans for owner-occupiers are not offered by Meridian Bank. IO is available only for investment property loans.

## 7. Loan Servicing & Account Management

### 7.1 Repayment Structure

Standard loan repayments are monthly, calculated on the outstanding principal balance using the reducing balance method. The standard repayment date is the 15th of each calendar month. Repayment frequency can be changed to fortnightly or weekly at the borrower's request (free of charge). The Bank may not unilaterally change the repayment date without the borrower's written consent.

### 7.2 Interest Calculation — Variable Rate

Interest on variable rate products accrues daily on the outstanding balance at the applicable daily rate (Annual Rate / 365). In months where a rate change occurs mid-cycle, the repayment may not fully cover the interest accrued, resulting in a temporary increase in the outstanding balance ('negative amortisation'). Borrowers must be notified of this effect within 2 business days of a rate change.

### 7.3 Grace Period & Late Payment

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grace period | 5 calendar days from scheduled repayment date |
| Late fee trigger | Day 6 past scheduled repayment date |
| Late payment fee | AUD $30 per event |
| Maximum late fees per 12-month period | AUD $180 (6 events) — Banking Code of Practice cap |
| Direct debit dishonour fee | AUD $15 per dishonour (separate to late fee) |
| Repayment date change | Once per 12-month period; AUD $50 administrative fee; must be requested in writing |

### 7.4 Acceptable Self-Service Changes (No Credit Re-Assessment)
- Contact details update (name change requires statutory declaration; address change requires address confirmation document)
- Bank account change for direct debit (mandate cancellation + new mandate registration; 5 business day processing window)
- Repayment frequency change (monthly to fortnightly or weekly, or reverse)
- Payoff quote request (valid for 30 days; must be system-generated)
- Loan statement request
- Interest certificate request (issued annually in July; ad hoc requests free of charge)
- Redraw request (where redraw facility is available; minimum redraw $1,000; processing time 1 business day)

### 7.5 Changes Requiring New Credit Assessment
- Tenure extension (maximum 30-year term applies; age at maturity policy applies)
- Loan top-up or limit increase
- Rate type switch (variable to fixed or fixed to variable at end of fixed period is automatic; mid-period switch to fixed requires new application)
- Addition of borrower to loan
- Removal of borrower from loan (serviceability must be re-assessed for remaining borrower(s) alone)
- Release of security (partial or full)
- Change from P&I to IO (only available for investment loans; requires re-assessment)

### 7.6 Fixed Rate Break Costs

**Break Cost = Outstanding Balance × (Fixed Rate – Current BBSW Rate for equivalent remaining term) × Remaining Fixed Term (in years)**

Where the current BBSW rate equals or exceeds the fixed rate, no break cost applies. Break cost calculations must be performed by Treasury and cannot be estimated by branch staff or provided verbally as binding quotes. A written break cost quote is valid for **5 business days**.

## 8. Fraud Detection & Financial Crime Prevention

### 8.1 Fraud Risk Framework

Meridian Bank is required under the AML/CTF Act to maintain an AML/CTF Program that includes transaction monitoring, suspicious matter reporting, and staff training. The Financial Crime team administers this program. All mortgage staff are required to complete annual AML/CTF training. A failure to identify and report a suspicious matter is a criminal offence.

### 8.2 Document Fraud Indicators

A single indicator must be documented and monitored. Two or more indicators in the same application require immediate escalation to the Financial Crime team.

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Font or formatting inconsistency | Text within a single document uses different fonts, sizes, or spacing — may indicate field-level digital editing |
| Income / industry mismatch | Claimed income materially inconsistent with industry salary benchmarks for the stated employer and role |
| Payroll deposit pattern anomalies | Bank statement deposits are exactly round numbers, arrive on atypical payroll dates, or show no variation across 3+ months |
| Statement metadata mismatch | PDF metadata (creation date, software) inconsistent with claimed statement period |
| Employer non-verification | Employer ABN is newly registered, has no web presence, no listed directors at ASIC, and no industry-consistent revenue |
| Address inconsistency | Residential address differs across identity document, bank statement, and ATO records |
| ABN age inconsistency | ABN registration date is more recent than claimed years in business |
| Circular deposits | Large deposits appear immediately before the bank statement window and are reversed or withdrawn immediately after |

### 8.3 Synthetic Identity Indicators

Two or more indicators in the same application require **immediate Financial Crime referral**.

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Thin file with high income | Equifax report shows fewer than 3 tradelines combined with a high income claim — inconsistent with claimed financial history |
| Tradeline clustering | All tradelines (credit cards, loans) opened within the same 4–6 month window — consistent with synthetic identity build-up |
| No electoral roll match | Name and address cannot be matched to the Australian Electoral Commission roll |
| Multiple recent hard enquiries | 5 or more Equifax hard enquiries in the past 60 days from multiple lenders |
| DVS failure | DVS verification of identity documents returns 'Not Matched' — elevated risk of fabricated documents |
| IP address clustering (broker) | Multiple applications from unrelated borrowers lodged via the same IP address or broker login within a short timeframe |

### 8.4 Fraud Escalation Procedure

1. Processing Officer identifies one or more fraud indicators.
2. Application is immediately placed in 'Financial Crime Review' status. No credit outcome (approval or decline) is communicated to the applicant or broker.
3. Processing Officer completes an Internal Fraud Referral (IFR) form and submits to the Financial Crime team within **2 business hours** of detection.
4. Financial Crime team responds within 1 business day: (a) Clear — application may proceed; (b) Enhanced Due Diligence required — specific additional documentation requested; (c) Decline on Financial Crime grounds.
5. If declined on Financial Crime grounds: Processing Officer communicates a standard decline without specifying fraud as the reason ('We are unable to assist with your lending needs at this time').
6. Financial Crime team determines whether an AUSTRAC Suspicious Matter Report (SMR) is required within **3 business days** of the Financial Crime determination.

> **⚠️ TIPPING OFF PROHIBITION** — Under the AML/CTF Act, it is an offence to inform an applicant or broker that a Suspicious Matter Report has been filed or is being considered. The standard decline language must be used. Staff must not indicate to the applicant that fraud is suspected. Violation of the tipping-off prohibition may result in criminal prosecution.

## 9. Financial Hardship & Arrears Management

### 9.1 Hardship Framework — Regulatory Basis

Meridian Bank is required under Part 4 of the National Credit Code (Schedule 1 to the NCCP Act) to consider all hardship applications from borrowers experiencing genuine financial difficulty. The Bank's obligations are:
- To assess the application honestly and with genuine consideration of the borrower's circumstances
- To respond to the borrower within **21 days** of receiving the hardship application
- To not take enforcement action (including issuing a notice of default or Statement of Claim) while a hardship application is under assessment
- To provide the borrower with written reasons if a hardship application is declined

### 9.2 Qualifying Hardship Triggers

| Trigger | Qualifying Status |
|---------|------------------|
| Involuntary unemployment | Genuine — evidence: redundancy letter, Centrelink confirmation |
| Illness or injury (borrower or dependent) | Genuine — evidence: medical certificate, hospital records |
| Relationship breakdown / separation | Genuine — evidence: statutory declaration; legal separation docs if available |
| Natural disaster impact | Genuine — evidence: insurance claim, government disaster declaration |
| Death of co-borrower or primary income earner | Genuine — evidence: death certificate |
| Temporary business income loss (self-employed) | Case-by-case — evidence: BAS showing revenue decline; accountant letter |
| Voluntary unemployment / career change | NOT qualifying — standard financial advice appropriate |
| Lifestyle-related over-commitment | NOT qualifying — refer to financial counselling |

### 9.3 Hardship Assistance Options

| Hardship Option | Details and Conditions |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Payment pause (moratorium) | Up to 3 months; interest capitalises; account moved to AASB 9 Stage 2; only available once per 5-year period per account |
| Reduced payment — Interest Only | Up to 6 months; reverts to P&I at end of IO period; account moved to Stage 2; eligible for owner-occupier loans (exception to IO restriction for hardship purposes only) |
| Tenure extension | Up to 2 years extension; must not cause age-at-maturity breach; formal variation agreement required |
| Capitalise arrears | Arrears added to principal; new repayment schedule issued; formal variation agreement required |
| Combination arrangement | Multiple options combined; requires Credit Manager approval |

> **⚠️ CRITICAL — Hardship and Collections Interaction** — Any account with a hardship application lodged (received but not yet assessed) OR with an active hardship arrangement must be FLAGGED in the Collections Management System. Collections Officers must check for active or pending hardship status **BEFORE** taking any collections action. Issuing a Letter of Demand, initiating an outbound collections call for payment, or filing any pre-legal documentation against an account with an active hardship application is a **breach of the NCCP Act** and constitutes unconscionable conduct. Non-compliance will result in ASIC referral and may attract civil penalties of up to **AUD $15.65 million per contravention**.

### 9.4 Collections — Days Past Due Framework

| DPD Bucket | Action Required | Responsibility |
|------------|----------------|----------------|
| 1–5 DPD | Grace period — no action; late fee applied on Day 6 | System automated |
| 6–15 DPD | SMS reminder + automated email notification | System automated; no manual action required |
| 16–30 DPD | Minimum 3 outbound call attempts; email notice — 'Payment Outstanding'; **hardship check mandatory before each contact** | Collections Officer |
| 31–60 DPD | Letter of Demand — Level 1; account review; **hardship check mandatory** | Collections Officer |
| 61–89 DPD | Letter of Demand — Level 2; hardship assessment offer must be made in writing | Collections Manager review; escalation recommendation |
| 90 DPD | APRA Stage 3 — Impaired; refer to Legal & Enforcement; NOI preparation begins | Head of Collections + Legal; Board reporting threshold |
| 91–119 DPD | Notice of Intent to Enforce Security issued (NCCP Act s88 requirement) | Legal team; 30-day response period to borrower |
| 120+ DPD | Statement of Claim preparation; property enforcement consideration | External solicitors; CCO notification |
| 180+ DPD | Write-off assessment; enforcement through to sale of security | CCO approval required for write-off |

### 9.5 Settlement Authority — Collections

| Role | Authority | Conditions |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Collections Officer | Fee and interest reduction up to 10% | Cannot reduce principal |
| Collections Manager | Fee and interest reduction up to 25%; principal reduction up to 5% | Formal offer letter required |
| Head of Collections | Total reduction up to 40%; principal haircut up to 15% | CCO notification required > 20% principal haircut |
| Chief Credit Officer | Full and final settlement; principal haircut above 15% | Board Risk Committee notification for write-offs > $500,000 |

## 10. Regulatory Compliance & Reporting

### 10.1 Responsible Lending — Post-Hayne Royal Commission Standards

- **Income must be independently verified** — reliance on borrower-declared income without document verification is not permitted.
- **Expenses must be genuinely assessed** — HEM is a floor, not a default. Where declared expenses exceed HEM, the declared figure must be used without adjustment.
- **'Liar loan' practices are prohibited** — where a Processing Officer has reason to doubt the accuracy of declared information, the application must be suspended and the borrower contacted for clarification. Proceeding on unverified information is a responsible lending breach.
- **The Bank must not recommend an unsuitable product** — if the credit assessment reveals that the proposed product is unsuitable for the borrower's objectives, financial situation, or needs, the Bank must not proceed with that product and must consider whether an alternative is suitable.

### 10.2 Suspicious Matter Reporting (SMR)

Meridian Bank must file an SMR with AUSTRAC within **3 business days** of forming a suspicion. The following transaction patterns must be escalated to Financial Crime for SMR assessment:
- Rapid full repayment of a mortgage from an unrelated third-party source with no documented connection to the borrower
- Request to direct loan proceeds to a third party unrelated to the property transaction
- Round-number cash deposits to an offset or transaction account immediately prior to settlement (structuring indicators)
- Borrower unable or unwilling to explain the source of a large deposit used toward the deposit or settlement
- Pattern of mortgage applications from multiple individuals sharing a residential address, IP address, or common broker
- Payoff immediately followed by refinancing to another lender with no apparent commercial rationale

> **⚠️ AUSTRAC TIPPING OFF — REPEATED FOR EMPHASIS** — Once a suspicion is formed and an SMR is under consideration or has been filed, no employee may disclose this to the subject of the report. This applies to applicants, borrowers, brokers, referrers, and any third party associated with the transaction. Violation is a criminal offence.

### 10.3 Fair Lending & Anti-Discrimination

Meridian Bank must not discriminate in credit decisions on the basis of any protected attribute including: race, colour, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, ethnic origin, marital status, pregnancy, or family responsibilities. All credit decisions must be defensible on objective, documented credit criteria.

The Compliance team conducts quarterly fair lending audits.

### 10.4 APRA Regulatory Reporting

| Return | Frequency | Relevance to Mortgage Portfolio |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------------|
| ARF 223.0 — Residential Mortgage | Monthly | Portfolio composition, LVR distribution, IO share, non-standard loans |
| ARF 110.0 — Capital Adequacy | Quarterly | Risk-weighted assets by exposure category including mortgage portfolio |
| ARF 220.0 — Credit Risk | Quarterly | Impaired assets, provisions, write-offs, Stage 2/3 movements |
| ARF 320.0 — Liquidity (LCR) | Monthly | Liquidity Coverage Ratio — retail deposit and mortgage outflow rates |
| ARS 701.0 — NSFR | Quarterly | Net Stable Funding Ratio — mortgage assets classified by maturity and quality |

### 10.5 Banking Code of Practice Obligations
- Minimum 30 days' notice of material changes to loan contract (rate changes must be communicated within 2 business days)
- Late fees capped at AUD $30 per event; total late fees in any 12-month period capped at AUD $180
- Financial hardship must be assessed genuinely and a response provided within 21 days
- Customers experiencing financial hardship will not be referred to debt collectors while a hardship application is under assessment
- A customer in financial hardship will be offered information about free financial counselling services

## 11. Loan Closure & Off-Boarding

### 11.1 Payoff Quote Process

- Payoff quote must be generated from the loan management system (not manually calculated) within **2 business days**.
- Must specify: outstanding principal, accrued interest to the nominated payout date, break costs (fixed rate loans), any outstanding fees, and the quote expiry date (**30 days**).
- Must be provided in writing (email or letter) and cannot be given verbally as a binding commitment.
- A new payoff quote must be obtained if the nominated payout date changes by more than 5 business days.

### 11.2 Full Repayment — Account Closure Process

1. Confirm receipt of full payout funds in the Bank's nominated settlement account.
2. Reconcile the amount received against the payoff quote (allow for daily interest accrual to actual settlement date).
3. If an overpayment is received: the excess must be refunded to the borrower's nominated account within 3 business days. Overpayments may not be applied as a principal reduction without the borrower's written instruction.
4. Loan account is marked **'CLOSED — PAID IN FULL'** in the system on the date funds are confirmed received.
5. Generate and dispatch No Due Certificate (NDC) within **5 business days** of account closure (see Section 11.4).
6. Initiate lien release process (see Section 11.3).

### 11.3 Lien Release — Mortgage Discharge

| Task | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Prepare Discharge of Mortgage | 2 business days from closure confirmation |
| Lodge Discharge at Land Titles Office | Within 14 calendar days from closure confirmation |
| Release title documents to borrower / solicitor | Within 5 business days; confirmation of receipt required |
| Notify LMI provider of loan closure (if LMI was in force) | Within 14 calendar days |
| Cancel property insurance held in Bank's name (if applicable) | Within 5 business days |
| PPSR discharge (if personal property registered) | Within 5 business days |
| Written confirmation of lien release to borrower | Simultaneously with title document release |

> **⚠️ TITLE DOCUMENTS — CUSTODY OBLIGATION** — Title documents held by Meridian Bank must be treated as legal instruments. They must only be released to the registered borrower, their nominated solicitor (with written authorisation), or as directed by a court order. Title documents must not be released pending confirmation of payout — the release process begins only after the account is confirmed CLOSED — PAID IN FULL.

### 11.4 No Due Certificate (NDC)

- Must be generated from the loan management system — manually prepared NDCs are not permitted.
- Must be on Meridian Bank letterhead and signed by an authorised officer (**Credit Manager level minimum**).
- Must contain: borrower full legal name(s), loan account number, property address (including title reference), date of final payment, statement that all obligations have been discharged, and the Bank's ABN and ACL number.
- Must be issued within **5 business days** of account closure.
- A copy must be retained in the Bank's document management system for **7 years**.

If the NDC contains an error, a corrected NDC must be issued within 2 business days of the error being identified. The original NDC must be marked as superseded.

## 12. Policy Exceptions

### 12.1 Exception Philosophy

This policy is designed to cover the full range of standard residential mortgage lending. Policy exceptions are permitted only where:
- The exception is genuinely justified by the specific circumstances of the borrower
- The risk of the exception is within the risk appetite of the delegated authority holder
- The exception is documented in writing with a clear rationale
- The exception does not result in a breach of any regulatory requirement (exceptions cannot be used to waive APRA buffer requirements, minimum KYC standards, or AML/CTF obligations)

### 12.2 Exception Logging & Reporting

All policy exceptions must be logged in the Exception Register maintained by Group Credit Risk. The register captures: loan account number, exception type, justification, approving delegate, and date. The CCO reviews the Exception Register monthly and reports to GCRC on exception trending.

### 12.3 Non-Excusable Breaches

The following are **NOT** policy exceptions — they are **prohibited conduct that cannot be authorised at any level**:

- Waiving or reducing the 3.0% APRA serviceability buffer
- Approving an application with an unresolved DVS mismatch
- Progressing an application while a fraud investigation is open
- Taking collections action against an account with an active hardship application
- Issuing an NDC before account closure is confirmed
- Providing a verbal binding payoff quote
- Disclosing to an applicant or broker that a Suspicious Matter Report has been filed
